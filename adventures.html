I Came Face-to-Face with an Alligator

Tampa, FL, 10/12/2019

Florida is famously home to thousands of alligators, some reaching upwards of 14 feet in length. On my many adventures I often encounter these scaly giants on the river's banks. However, on rare occasions, I have a more intimate face to face encounter underwater while diving in blackwater. I can remember the first time it happened, and the animal's eye focused in on my own and I could tell it didn't care that humans were at the top of the food chain. For millions of years we existed somewhere in the middle and that moment reminded me of that chilling fact.

Are Sharks Teeth Radioactive?

St. Petersburg, FL, 8/14/2019

Are sharks teeth radioactive? Well the answer is not straight forward... There are millions of tons of radioactive material naturally dissolved in the ocean at any given time. Fossil shark teeth that are millions of years old can be preserved with trace amounts of this radioactive material. However, how radioactive a sharks tooth is depends on the location and age of the tooth. Bone Valley, Florida is famous for radioactive gypsum stacks from fossil shark tooth formations but the teeth themselves are rather benign. But travel into Georgia and some teeth will tip the scale on a gieger counter (not at harmful levels).
